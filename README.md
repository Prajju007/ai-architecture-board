# AI Engineering Board

## Vision

The AI Engineering Board is a structured multi-agent decision system designed to improve engineering decisions through disciplined reasoning, challenge, review, and documentation.

Rather than relying on a single LLM response, the system creates a formal decision-making process where multiple AI agents analyze a problem, challenge assumptions, review trade-offs, and arrive at a documented recommendation.

The goal is not to replace engineers.

The goal is to create a rigorous engineering review process before implementation begins.

---

# Problem Statement

Traditional AI workflows produce solutions immediately.

This often leads to:

* Premature solutioning
* Over-engineering
* Missing assumptions
* Poorly documented decisions
* Inconsistent architectural reasoning

The AI Engineering Board introduces structured engineering governance into AI-assisted decision making.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. AI is an Amplifier, Not an Oracle

The system assists engineering decisions.

The human remains the final decision-maker.

---

## 2. Deterministic Governance

Consensus is never determined by an LLM.

Approval logic is deterministic and transparent.

---

## 3. Artifact-Centric Design

The system focuses on producing and improving engineering artifacts.

Artifacts matter more than agents.

Examples:

* PRDs
* Decision Contexts
* Architecture Proposals
* Reviews
* ADRs

---

## 4. First Principles Reasoning

Agents must understand:

* Goals
* Constraints
* Trade-offs
* Assumptions
* Risks

before proposing solutions.

---

## 5. Challenge Before Execution

Every proposal should be reviewed and challenged before implementation.

# Design Decisions

## Why LangGraph?

LangGraph provides:

* State management
* Workflow orchestration
* Conditional routing
* Future human approval points

without building custom orchestration.

---

## Why PRD First?

Architectures should be generated from a structured problem statement rather than a free-form prompt.

---

## Why Deterministic Consensus?

LLMs are poor arbiters of agreement.

Approval criteria should be transparent and reproducible.

---

## Why Human-in-the-Loop?

Engineering accountability remains with humans.

The board assists decisions but does not own them.

while maintaining transparency and governance throughout the engineering lifecycle.

# Latest Architectural Decision (V3)

## Decision

The board will evolve from a traditional Architect → Reviewer workflow into a structured multi-model engineering deliberation system.

Previous approach:

PRD
↓
Problem Framer
↓
Decision Context
↓
Architect
↓
Reviewer
↓
Consensus

New approach:

PRD
↓
Problem Framer
↓
Decision Context
↓
GPT Initial Position

Gemini Initial Position
↓
Deliberation Round 1
↓
Deliberation Round 2
↓
Deliberation Round 3
↓
GPT Final Position

Gemini Final Position
↓
Claude Synthesis
↓
Consensus Artifact
↓
Human Decision

## Rationale

The previous architecture assigned fixed roles to models:

* GPT = Architect
* Gemini = Reviewer

This constrained Gemini to reviewing the architecture proposed by GPT rather than contributing independent architectural reasoning.

The board's objective is not review alone. The objective is to explore architectural trade-offs through multiple engineering perspectives.

To achieve this, models will participate as independent architects rather than fixed reviewers.

## Deliberation Principles

### Independent Thinking First

GPT and Gemini generate their initial positions independently before seeing each other's reasoning.

This prevents anchoring bias and encourages diverse architectural options.

### Collaborative Deliberation

The deliberation process is not a debate.

Models should:

* Identify agreements
* Identify disagreements
* Surface assumptions
* Surface trade-offs
* Highlight missing information
* Update their positions when appropriate

Models should not attempt to "win" an argument.

### Bounded Deliberation

The discussion is limited to three rounds.

This prevents endless agent loops and unnecessary token consumption.

### Claude as Synthesizer

Claude does not act as a judge.

Claude receives the complete deliberation transcript and produces:

* Agreements
* Disagreements
* Open Questions
* Decision Drivers
* Recommendation

### Human Decision Authority

The final decision always remains with the human operator.

The board assists engineering decisions but does not own them.

## Expected Benefits

* Independent architectural thinking
* Reduced anchoring bias
* Richer trade-off exploration
* Better engineering discussions
* More realistic architecture review board behavior
* Stronger foundation for future ADRs, memory, and organizational learning
